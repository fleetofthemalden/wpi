WPI Men's Rowing Welcomes Coach Ross and Coach Minnick

Apr 01, 2016

Worcester, MA --- The WPI men's rowing team welcome two new Assistant Coaches joining The Engineers for the Fall 2016 rowing season.  Earlier this February the WPI Board of Trustees unanimously approved the addition of two new part-time coaching staff. Head Coach Larry Noble announced the selection this week of former Tufts University rowers William Ross (Tufts A'14), and Dixon Minnick (Tufts A'15) to fill the spots. 

"We're really excited about Will Ross and [Dixon] Minnick and what they have to offer in terms of youth and growing our sculling program," Coach Noble explained.  Minnick and Ross, both from the same novice rowing class, were instrumental in pioneering fall sculling as rowers at Tufts University. 

'Sculling' is a type of rowing in which each oarsman use 2 oars whereas 'sweeping' is a type of rowing in which the oarsman has one oar each.  Traditionally WPI Men's Rowing has been dominant in sweeping but neglected sculling. 

"I have some big aspirations for the novice boats," said Ross, who will be joining Coach Bryan Purcell in coaching the Novice squad. "I'd really like to introduce more cross-fit into the winter training regiment to prevent these athletes from burning out in their first year."  Ross also says he plans to move from 'steady-state' training to more 'anaerobic-threshhold' training in order to maximize boat speed.

Minnick has his own plans as he prepares to work with the 3rd and 4th Varsity eights.  "I was never the fastest guy on the team--in fact I was pretty damn slow on the erg," explained Minnick. "I always had to rely on technique to seat-race my way into the [Head of the] Charles boat or the 2V, so I really want to make strong technique the foundation of our program to give these guys a chance when they get stronger." 

When asked about the prospect of Tufts Crew facing WPI squads coached by Tufts rowing alumni, Ross and Minnick's fellow teammate, Spencer Moslow responded, "I don't like it. They're both pricks." 

WPI men's rowing opens the season hosting Wesleyan, Colby and UMass on Saturday, April 2nd.
